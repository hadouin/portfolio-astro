## Overview

Monquiro is an AI-powered accounting tool that eliminates manual invoice entry by automatically detecting invoices in Gmail, extracting their data, and matching them to entries inside Pennylane. I designed the brand identity and logo system that supports this positioning.

## The Brand

Monquiro targets solo operators, small teams, and growing businesses that are tired of manual bookkeeping. The brand needed to feel:

- **Trustworthy** — it handles financial data and accounting workflows
- **Efficient** — the product's entire promise is "stop manual data entry"
- **Modern** — AI-first, purpose-built for the Gmail + Pennylane stack
- **Approachable** — not a cold enterprise accounting tool

## Logo System

The mark balances a clean wordmark with a distinctive symbol that reads well at small sizes (favicon, app icon, email signatures) and scales up cleanly for marketing pages and social assets. The type choice leans practical over aspirational, mirroring the product's direct messaging ("Stop manual data entry").

## Deliverables

- Primary logo and wordmark
- App icon / favicon
- Brand banner for the marketing site
- Color palette and typography guidelines
- Social preview and OG image assets

## Product Context

Monquiro's feature set that the brand supports:

- Automated Gmail invoice import with duplicate detection
- AI-powered matching to Pennylane entries with confidence scoring
- A financial data chatbot for natural-language queries
- Multi-email account support with daily auto-sync
- Pricing tiers from free up to €99/month, covering freelancers through mid-sized companies

## What I Learned

Designing for a focused B2B SaaS product reinforced how much restraint matters. The logo doesn't need to explain the whole product — it needs to earn trust quickly and stay out of the way while the software does the work.
